Exhibit A · The Billing-Rate Mismatch

The average lawyer bills 3 hours of an 8-hour day. Routine work is where the rest goes.

That’s not an opinion; it’s the industry’s own data.* Review stacks, research memos, first drafts: necessary work, but every hour your best people spend on it is premium capacity you can never bill again.

A.1, The backlog

Matters wait. Clients notice.

Discovery piles up, contracts queue, and turnaround stretches from days to weeks. The work that wins clients gets crowded out by the work that merely keeps them.

A.2, The margin bleed

Billed twice, shaved twice.

Firms invoice only 88% of the hours they log, and collect just 93% of what they invoice.* Routine work is where clients push back hardest, so those hours get written down, written off, or eaten.

A.3, The hiring trap

Headcount is a fixed cost. Caseloads aren’t.

A paralegal’s median salary is $62,890, over $80,000 fully loaded with benefits.** Hire for the peak and you carry that through every trough. Staff for the trough and every busy month becomes a fire drill.

Exhibit G · Anticipated Objections

Asked before you reach out.

Are you replacing my in-house counsel or paralegals?+

No, we extend your team’s capacity; we don’t replace its judgment. Legal strategy and final sign-off stay with your firm. We absorb the high-volume execution underneath it, and every deliverable is reviewed by a supervising lawyer before it reaches you.

How is client confidentiality maintained?+

A mutual NDA is executed before anything is shared. Documents are accessible only to the assigned engagement team, access is limited and monitored, and no client materials are run through unvetted AI tools. Work is handled to U.S. state and federal data protection standards.

Do you work with solo attorneys and small firms?+

Yes, there’s no minimum firm size and no retainer. Many of our engagements start as a single contract review or research memo from a solo practitioner. The process is identical whether you send one document or a monthly caseload.

What jurisdictions do you support?+

U.S. federal and all state jurisdictions. Research is jurisdiction-specific, documents follow U.S. court formatting, and the supervising lawyer checks jurisdictional context before delivery. We’re registered in Sheridan, Wyoming.

How fast is turnaround?+

Turnaround is set per engagement and confirmed in writing before work begins, typically days, not weeks, depending on volume. Because scope and deadline are fixed up front, there’s no ambiguity about when work comes back.

What billing models do you offer?+

Two: a fixed fee per assignment (quoted before you commit), or ongoing monthly capacity for firms with recurring volume. No retainers to start, no minimums, and no lock-in. Ongoing clients continue month to month because the work earns it.

What if the work isn’t up to our standard?+

Then the first assignment is free, that’s the Attorney-Ready Guarantee. If the first deliverable isn’t fit for attorney sign-off by your firm’s standard, the invoice is void and you keep the work product. We can offer that because a practicing lawyer gates every deliverable before it leaves.
