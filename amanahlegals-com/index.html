Exhibit A · The Paralegal Capacity Problem

Every firm needs paralegal support. Most can’t afford the overhead that comes with it.

Intake, drafting, research, discovery — work that doesn’t need a bar card to execute. But U.S. paralegal headcount is a fixed cost that doesn’t flex with your caseload, and the alternatives come with their own risks.

A.1, The overhead trap

Headcount is fixed. Caseloads aren’t.

A U.S. paralegal costs $62,890 median salary — over $80,000 fully loaded with benefits, PTO, and training.** Hire for the peak and you carry that cost through every trough. Staff for the trough and every busy month becomes a fire drill.

A.2, The quality gap

Temp paralegals come with no guarantee.

Staffing agencies charge a 25–40% markup and offer no quality assurance. Work lands on your desk unreviewed. Turnover is high. Every replacement means another round of onboarding, software training, and explaining your standards from scratch.

A.3, The attorney time leak

Routine work at premium rates is the biggest hole in the bucket.

When no paralegal support is available, attorneys absorb the overflow — review stacks, research memos, first drafts. Every hour spent that way is billable capacity that disappears into work a paralegal should have handled.

Exhibit G · Anticipated Objections

Asked before you reach out.

Are you replacing my in-house counsel or paralegals?+

No, we extend your team’s capacity; we don’t replace its judgment. Legal strategy and final sign-off stay with your firm. We absorb the high-volume execution underneath it, and every deliverable is reviewed by a supervising lawyer before it reaches you.

How is client confidentiality maintained?+

A mutual NDA is executed before anything is shared. Documents are accessible only to the assigned engagement team, access is limited and monitored, and no client materials are run through unvetted AI tools. Work is handled to U.S. state and federal data protection standards.

Do you work with solo attorneys and small firms?+

Yes, there’s no minimum firm size and no retainer. Many of our engagements start as a single contract review or research memo from a solo practitioner. The process is identical whether you send one document or a monthly caseload.

How fast can a paralegal be arranged?+

Once we complete the 20-minute strategy call and execute the NDA, we identify and brief your matched paralegal within 24 hours. In most cases your paralegal is ready to begin work the next business day. We don’t start the clock until you confirm they’re the right fit.

What billing models do you offer?+

We bill by the hour for dedicated paralegal time, with rates confirmed in writing before you commit to anything. For firms with consistent volume, we offer a monthly retained-hours package at a reduced rate. There is no minimum commitment to get started — the 15-hour trial is billed only if you’re satisfied. No retainers, no lock-in.

What if the work isn’t up to our standard?+

Two things happen. First, the 15-hour trial is free — if you aren’t completely thrilled with the quality of your paralegal’s intake, drafting, or research, the invoice is void and you keep the work product. Second, if quality issues arise at any point after the trial, we replace your paralegal at no cost to you. No arguments, no delays.
